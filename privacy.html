Privacy Policy

Effective date: 29 April 2026 · Last updated: 29 April 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what personal information we collect when you use the Stokmate app and website (the "Service"), why we collect it, how we use and protect it, and the rights you have over it under the Protection of Personal Information Act, 4 of 2013 ("POPIA") and, where applicable, the EU/UK General Data Protection Regulation ("GDPR").

By creating an account or using Stokmate, you confirm that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy.

2. What Stokmate does, and what it does not do

Stokmate is a software tool that helps savings groups (stokvels, pardners, sou-sous, tontines, burial societies and similar groups) track contributions, communicate with members, log activity, and generate records.

Important: Stokmate never holds, receives, or disburses your group's money. We are not a bank, financial services provider, or payment institution. All money continues to flow directly between members and the group's chosen account holder. We only record what members tell us about contributions and payouts.

3. Personal information we collect

3.1 Information you give us

  • Account information: name, email address, password (stored as a one-way hash), and optionally your phone number.
  • Group information: group name, group type, contribution amount, currency, cycle, member list, and invite codes you create.
  • Activity data: payments you log, members you confirm, dispute notes, and other entries you add to the group's activity log.
  • Subscription information: if you upgrade to Pro, our payment processor (PayFast) collects your payment details directly. We receive a confirmation token and your subscription status — we never see your card number.
  • Support communications: messages you send us by email or in-app.

3.2 Information collected automatically

  • Technical data: device type, browser, operating system, IP address, language, and timestamps.
  • Usage data: pages visited, features used, error logs.
  • Cookies and similar technologies: see Section 9.

3.3 Information you provide about other people

When you invite members to a group, you may provide their name, phone number, or email address so the app can issue an invite. You confirm that you have permission from those people to share their information with us for that purpose. Once they accept the invite and create an account, they become responsible for their own information.

6. Cross-border transfers

Some of our service providers (in particular Supabase, Netlify, and Twilio) process personal information outside the Republic of South Africa. Where we transfer your information across borders, we rely on lawful transfer mechanisms permitted by section 72 of POPIA — typically that the recipient is subject to laws or binding agreements that provide an adequate level of protection. Where a recipient is in a country whose laws are not deemed adequate, we ensure that contractual safeguards (such as standard data-protection clauses) are in place.

7. How long we keep your information

  • Active accounts: for as long as your account exists.
  • Closed accounts: we delete or anonymise your personal information within 90 days of account closure, except where we must keep it longer to comply with legal, accounting, tax, or audit obligations (typically up to 5 years).
  • Group records: while a group is active, group records are retained so members can rely on them. If you leave a group, your historical contributions remain visible to other members for the integrity of the group's records, but your contact details are removed from the active member list.
  • Backups: encrypted backups may be retained for up to 30 days after deletion.

8. How we protect your information

  • All traffic between your device and Stokmate is encrypted using HTTPS/TLS.
  • Passwords are stored as one-way hashes — we never see your actual password.
  • Access to the database is restricted by row-level security: users can only see information from groups they belong to.
  • Service-provider access is restricted, logged, and reviewed.
  • We follow recognised security practices and maintain reasonable, appropriate, technical and organisational measures as required by section 19 of POPIA.

If a security compromise affects your personal information, we will notify you and the Information Regulator as required by section 22 of POPIA.

9. Cookies and similar technologies

Stokmate uses a small number of cookies and similar local-storage technologies — strictly necessary ones to keep you signed in, remember your preferences, and operate the Service. We do not use third-party advertising cookies. You can clear cookies in your browser at any time, but doing so may sign you out and reset preferences.

10. Your rights under POPIA

You have the following rights with respect to your personal information:

  • Right of access: request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Right to correction: ask us to correct information that is inaccurate, irrelevant, excessive, out of date, incomplete, misleading, or unlawfully obtained.
  • Right to deletion: ask us to delete your account and personal information, subject to the retention rules in Section 7.
  • Right to object: object to processing based on legitimate interest, including direct marketing.
  • Right to withdraw consent: where we rely on consent, you can withdraw it at any time. This does not affect processing already carried out.
  • Right to lodge a complaint: you can complain to the Information Regulator (South Africa) — see Section 13.

To exercise any of these rights, email hello@stokmate.co.za. We respond within a reasonable time, usually within 30 days.

11. Children

Stokmate is not intended for people under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us so we can delete it.
